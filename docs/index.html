Insomniac

Keep your Mac awake — even with the lid closed — without cooking it.

Download for macOS Version 1.0 · macOS 14 (Sonoma) or later · Apple Silicon & Intel

What it does

One switch in your menu bar. Smart safety underneath.

💻

Lid-closed stay-awake

Flip one switch and your Mac keeps running with the lid shut — perfect for downloads, renders, or an external display.

⏱️

Auto-off timer

Pick a duration and it turns itself off automatically, so you never forget and drain the battery.

🌡️

Thermal & battery safety

Stops on its own if your Mac gets too hot or the battery runs low — it protects your hardware.

✨

Smart advice

Suggests a safe duration based on heat, power source, and system load before you start.

Set up in 3 steps

Takes about a minute. The Terminal step is needed only once.

1

Download & install

Open the downloaded insomniac.dmg, then drag the Insomniac icon onto the Applications folder. You can eject the disk image afterward.

2

Allow it to open (one time)

Because this is a free test build (not yet signed through Apple's paid program), macOS blocks it at first. To allow it, open Terminal (press ⌘ Space, type “Terminal”, hit Return) and paste this command, then press Return:

xattr -dr com.apple.quarantine /Applications/insomniac.app

Now open Insomniac from your Applications folder. It’ll launch normally from then on.

3

Find it & use it

Insomniac has no Dock icon — it lives in the menu bar at the top-right of your screen. Look for the 🌙 moon icon (a welcome guide will point right at it on first launch). Click it, flip Stay Awake on, and when prompted, enter your Mac password — that’s the normal macOS approval.

Is the Terminal step safe? Yes. That command just removes the “downloaded from the internet” quarantine flag macOS adds to any file from a browser or AirDrop. It’s the standard way to run apps that haven’t gone through Apple’s $99/yr notarization yet. Insomniac collects no data and has no network tracking.
